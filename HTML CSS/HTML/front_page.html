What is organic farming?

Organic farming is a system that avoids the use of synthetic inputs like pestisides, fertilizers, hormones, etc. and relyng on techniques like crop rotation, organic wastes, farm manure, rock additives and crop residues for plant protection and nutrient utilization.

How Organic Farming Benefits Human Health

Pesticides may cause various types cancer in human bodies. And also many studies has shown that it may also cause many other diseases(non-cancerous) such as "neurologic, reproductive, and gentoxic effects."
Organic Farming is also usefull for Farmworkers. As they are the forefront of pesticide exposure and their destructive health effects. In conventional agriculture, farmworkers prepare huge amounts of toxic chemicals, apply them to the crops, and manage sprayed crops. They are exposed to much higher concentrations of pesticides than consumers are by ingresting the end products.
Excluding all these deffects organic foods contain more antioxidants, polyphenols, essential fatty acids, and other vital nutrients.
Organic agriculture encourages the use of sustainable farming methods and discourages the use of toxic chemicals; as result, it can:
.Reduce air,water, and soil pollution
.Save water and energy
.Build healthy nutrient-rich soil
.Protect animal welfare and biodiversity
